King Cobra

The King Cobra (Ophiophagus hannah) is the world's longest venomous snake, reaching up to 18 feet in length. Known for its majestic hood and powerful presence, it primarily feeds on other snakes. Despite its fearsome reputation, it typically avoids human contact and will only attack if threatened. Native to forests and grasslands of Southeast Asia, the King Cobra plays a vital role in controlling local ecosystems. Its distinctive hiss and impressive hood display are key defense mechanisms, making it both a fascinating and formidable predator in the wild.
