Blue whales are the largest animals to have ever existed, reaching a maximum length of 98 ft and weighing up to 199 tons.

They are called blue whales because of their coloring. Despite appearing grey on the surface, underwater they appear bright blue due to how light interacts with the water.

They have big hearts. In fact, the major blood vessel of their heart is so big that a human baby could crawl through it.
