Parasitic Jaegers breed in the Arctic but, like the related gulls, live on the ocean. However, unlike gulls, these birds live a more “piratical lifestyle” and use their sharp wings and quick speed to attack other birds of the sea. They often attack in groups and are relentless in their chases. These birds are also known from stealing from other birds, and it is from this kleptoparasitic behavior that their name was born.
