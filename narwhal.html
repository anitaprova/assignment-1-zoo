Narwhals, also called the ‘unicorns of the sea’, are creatures known for their horn-like tusks that protrude from their heads. These tusks are actually an enlarged tooth with over 10,000 nerve endings and a height of up to 10 ft. The diet of a narwhal mostly consists of Greenland halibut and Arctic and polar cod, squid and shrimp. Interestingly, the color of their skin changes as they age, with newborns being blue-gray, juveniles black-blue, adults a mottled-gray, and old narwhals being almost completely white.
