Caribou, also called Reindeer, are mammals of the deer family characterized by their scoop-shaped hooves which they use to dig for food. Unlike other deer-types, this animal is medium-sized and grows to about 3.5 ft and also has a heavier weight of about 175-400 pounds. Caribou are migratory animals and travel north in the summer, where they enjoy a diet of mushrooms, plants, and grass in the tundra, and then south in the winter. Over the course of one year the Caribou travels over 1600 miles, making its journey one of the largest animal migrations on the planet.
